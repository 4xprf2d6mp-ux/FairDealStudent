About FairDeal Student

As a team of money-conscious students, we came together with one shared goal: to make the real cost of student life visible before you sign anything.

Our CEO, Rami Rantanen, first noticed this problem while working as a salesperson for mobile plans and related services. Day after day he watched students walk up to different stands from the big three and receive completely different prices for almost identical contracts. The cheapest deals were never shown online only offered over the counter.

2022: First stand, first idea

It all began when Rami noticed at work that almost all students were paying two to four times more than necessary for a mobile plan. Even when he suggested switching to a more affordable option, most did not bother, because they didn‚Äôt understand how simple the process really is. At work Rami started questioning students about their overall monthly budget ‚Äì he was shocked.

2022: From questions to FairDeal Student

Rami shared his findings with friends, and together we turned them into a simple spreadsheet. We started collecting prices of contracts, photos of stand posters and anonymous bills from students. This spreadsheet became the first version of FairDeal Student, and within three months it had gained recognition and widespread use across our campus.

2023: The AI leap and a major challenge

In 2023 we decided to scale up. We implemented AI tools to help us scan publicly available offers, student campaigns and Reddit discussions and combined this with the prices we observed at physical stands.

Our transparency, however, was not welcomed by everyone. Six months later, the three largest telecom companies challenged our work legally, arguing that publishing detailed comparisons of their offers was unfair.

2023‚Äì2024: Standing our ground

The case forced us to refine our methods, double-check sources and clearly explain that we use only publicly available or voluntarily submitted information. With support from student unions and legal advisors, we demonstrated that our comparisons were neutral, educational and in the public interest. In the end, we won. The challenge that almost shut us down instead made FairDeal Student more reliable and resilient.

Today and the future

Today FairDeal Student has a solid presence within the student community across Finland. We remain a student-run, non-profit project dedicated to making contracts understandable and costs transparent. As we look ahead, we plan to expand our guides to more cities and new expense categories, continuing our mission to ensure that no student has to overpay simply because the best offer was hidden at the wrong stand on the wrong day.

How to Use FairDeal Student

  1. Open the Cost Tracker.

    Start on the home page and click on the section that matches your situation (for example ‚ÄúFirst apartment‚Äù, ‚ÄúShared apartment‚Äù or ‚ÄúAlready living on my own‚Äù). This brings you to the main cost overview.

  2. Choose your city and housing type.

    Select the city or region where you live or plan to move, and pick the housing type that is closest to yours. The page updates to show typical rent, electricity and internet ranges for that choice.

  3. Check the contract benchmarks.

    Scroll down to see our current student price ranges for mobile plans, home insurance and electricity contracts. Each category includes a short explanation and a simple comparison table.

  4. Enter what you currently pay.

    With your latest bills or contracts in front of you, type your monthly amounts into the fields next to our benchmarks. If a number turns red, it means you are well above the common student range.

  5. Estimate your yearly saving.

    The page shows how much you could save per month and per year if you moved to a realistic student-level contract. This helps you see immediately whether it is worth switching.

  6. Follow the switching guide.

    Click on ‚ÄúHow to switch‚Äù under each category. You will find short scripts and checklists for chatting or calling providers, including which questions to ask and which fees to check.

  7. Review and update.

    After you have changed a contract, return to FairDeal Student, update your numbers and track how much you are saving. Repeat this once or twice a year to keep your bills under control.
